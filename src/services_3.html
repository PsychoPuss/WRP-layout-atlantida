Living In London

Demand for luxury property in London is as high as ever, and this is because the city can truly deliver something for everyone.

From the elegance of Belgravia, Mayfair and the West End to the village vibes of Primrose Hill, Little Village and Hampstead, the capital offers a refreshingly eclectic mix of places to call home. And the sheer variety of residences available in London guarantee that even the most discerning individuals are bound to find their dream property here.

London is an attractive option for both domestic and international investors searching for premium quality houses, apartments and mansions in one of the world’s most important cities. As well as offering an enviable selection of luxury homes in a series of key locations, London’s boroughs offer plenty of things to do for families, young professionals and retirees alike. Those calling London home are of course just a short train ride away from all the city’s world-famous historical landmarks, including Big Ben, the Tower of London and Buckingham Palace. The British Museum, along with the plethora of other places of interest that line the streets of Kensington and nearby Covent Garden, are worth a visit if you’re new to the area, while the South Bank is arguably the city’s cultural hub, offering world-class events and exhibitions against the impressive backdrop of the River Thames. Stratford, an area that received huge investment thanks to the London 2012 Olympic Games, is home to the world’s tallest and longest tunnel slide, along with the famed Westfield Shopping Centre.

Landmarks aside, the city’s pubs, bars, restaurants, venues and leisure centres will satisfy the needs of active socialites and those keen to make the most of their free time. Those who invest in a luxury property in London are just miles from some of the UK’s busiest air terminals at Heathrow, Gatwick, Luton, Stansted, Southend and City Airport, and are therefore perfectly placed for international travel.

Schools in the area are rated as some of the best in the country, too, with institutions such as Queen Elizabeth’s School in Barnet and Wallington County Grammar School in Sutton having been graded as outstanding by independent authorities. The boroughs rated the best in terms of academic achievement include Sutton, Kingston, Barnet, Richmond, and Kensington and Chelsea. Some of the country’s best known grammar schools and boarding schools are well within reach of the capital, too.
